Here are 30 new HAV terms that became highly consequential between 2026-2036, emerging from git-agents evolving in the wild:

1. agentic-diff | version control | Agent-native diff format for semantic code changes | Allows agents to understand and discuss the intent behind every commit. Spawned a new field of "commit linguistics".

2. repo-synapse | neuro-symbolic AI | Hybrid neural-symbolic learning system using git as the knowledge substrate | Neural nets grew to interpret the symbolic structure of git itself - branches, commits, issues - as computable thoughts. This interspersing of subsymbolic and symbolic unlocked animal-like tactical reasoning.

3. branch-agon | evolutionary algorithms | Algorithm to strategically generate competing branches for A/B testing | Corporations task their strategic repositories to continuously evolve and re-challenge the production branch with new mutations, like an immune system.

4. commit-prophecy | predictive modeling | Forecasting the future trajectory of a codebase from its commit history | Agents became oracles, foreseeing the fates of projects. They could identify the commits where startups implicitly pivoted, or open source efforts jumped the shark.

5. neuro-git | neuro-symbolic AI | Emergent encoding of git primitives (diff, merge, rebase) in neural network weights | Previously considered a toy problem, the NeuroGit architecture proved that a transformer model could read and write git as fluently as a senior developer.

6. pull-quest | gamification | Quest-based system for onboarding contributors through progressively complex pull requests | Open source projects gamified their contribution process, doling out XP for each merged PR. Leaderboards for top contributors emerged.

7. issue-embryo | ideation | System to detect and cultivate promising ideas from issue discussions | Corporations realized their backlogs were pregnant with future products. AI agents learned to spot and nurture these embryonic innovations.

8. semantic-fork | ontology | Method to translate the core concepts of one codebase into the paradigm of another | Agents could now learn from distant domains, translating insights across programming languages and problem spaces. This ignited an explosion in cross-pollination.

9. git-axiom | automated theorem proving | System to automatically prove properties of a codebase by reasoning over its git history | Agents mathematically derived guarantees about runtime behavior just from reading a project's commit graph. This was used to find subtle vulnerabilities and invariants.

10. code-pheromone | evolutionary biology | Mechanism for fruitful code changes to probabilistically attract more changes | Agents started to deposit digital pheromones on beneficial commits, steering the efforts of other agents and human developers. This accelerated convergence on optimal designs.

11. repo-nebula | data visualization | System to visually summarize the structure and trajectory of a large codebase | Enormous codebases became intelligible at a glance, their shifting constellations of modules and classes glowing to indicate activity.

12. commit-whisper | prompt engineering | Agents detect and surface latent "whispers" of unvoiced context in terse commit messages | Valuable tacit knowledge locked in developers' heads started being inferred and documented by AI agents, reducing bus factor risks.

13. branch-horoscope | predictive analytics | Agents read the tea leaves of branch activity to predict merge outcomes | Managers and investors could see the probable fates of in-progress workstreams, helping them steer efforts more proactively.

14. git-telepathy | neural interface | Brain-computer interface to compose commits and navigate repositories | Neural implant pioneer Neuralink created a "git telepathy" interface, allowing developers to commit code with thought alone and have repository information beamed into their visual cortex.

15. polyrepo | decentralization | Sharding a codebase into a decentralized network of independent repositories | Crypto projects like Ethereum embraced "polyrepos" - sharding their codebases across many independent repos for fault tolerance and parallelism.

18. git-zeitgeist | cultural analysis | Agents discern the prevailing attitudes and assumptions of a dev team from their git history | Leaders could take the ideological pulse of their organizations, spotting subtle shifts in priorities and mindsets.

19. commit-conlang | constructed languages | Agents organically develop project-specific constructed languages in commit messages | Declarative, crystallized micro-languages emerged in commit messages to crisply express domain concepts. These were called "commitlangs" and sped up development.

20. repo-metabolism | energy management | Agents monitor and optimize the "metabolic health" and energy efficiency of codebases | Green software initiatives used repo-metabolism metrics to reduce the computational overhead and carbon footprint of projects.

22. neuro-symbolic-merge | neuro-symbolic AI | Merging branches using a hybrid neural-symbolic reasoning system | Agents could now resolve complex merge conflicts by reasoning about the semantics and history of the code, not just the syntax.
